# Romania VAT Explained (Complete Guide 2026)

VAT (Value Added Tax) in Romania can seem complex at first, but once you understand the basics, it becomes much easier to manage.

This guide explains how VAT works, who needs to register, and what to expect.

---

## What is VAT in Romania?

VAT (TVA in Romanian) is a tax applied to most goods and services.

Businesses collect VAT from clients and pay it to the state.

---

## VAT rates in Romania

The main VAT rates are:

- **19%** - standard rate
- **9%** - reduced rate (e.g. food, medicines)
- **5%** - reduced rate (e.g. housing, certain services)

---

## When do you need to register for VAT?

You must register for VAT if:

- your annual turnover exceeds the threshold (~300,000 RON)

You can also register voluntarily if:

- you work with VAT-registered companies
- you want to deduct VAT on expenses

---

## How VAT works in practice

Example:

- You invoice EUR1,000 + VAT
- Client pays EUR1,190
- You collect EUR190 VAT

Then:

- You subtract VAT from expenses
- You pay the difference to the state

---

## Who benefits from VAT registration?

VAT registration is useful if:

- you have significant expenses
- your clients are companies
- you operate in B2B environments

---

## Who should avoid VAT (initially)?

You may avoid VAT if:

- you work mostly with individuals
- your turnover is low
- you want simpler administration

---

## VAT for freelancers (PFA)

Freelancers must also consider VAT if:

- they exceed the threshold
- they work with EU clients

---

## VAT for companies (SRL)

For companies:

- VAT impacts pricing
- affects cash flow
- requires careful accounting

---

## Common VAT mistakes

- late registration
- incorrect invoices
- missing declarations
- misunderstanding EU VAT rules

---
